VICTORIAN SKILLS DATA ANALYSIS

This page provides an analysis of data taken from the Victorian Employment Projections Dashboard provided by the Victorian Skills Authority.

Start scrolling to begin the analysis.

In 2024 over 3.6 million Victorians were employed in a variety of occupations. The bubbles on the right each represent a different industry, with the size of the bubble representing the number of people employed in that industry.

The largest sector in terms of employment was the Health Care and Social Assistance sector, with 570,000 people employed.

Health Care is not just large in 2024, it is also expected to be one of the fastest growing sectors in Victoria with an annual growth rate of 2.8% between 2024 and 2027.

This is only outmatched by the Construction sector, which is expected to grow at a rate of 4.2% between 2024 and 2027.

The graph on the right show the number of new workers entering each industry in 2027 compared to the total number of national job vacancies.

Given that the vacancy data presented is at a national level, it is interesting that a pretty linear relationship exists between the vacancy data and the expected employment growth.

Some exceptions to this are Construction, Accommodation and food services and Education and training.

Let's take a closer look at Health Care and Social Assistance.

This plot shows the income distribution for workers in Health Care and Social Assistance. as of the 2021 Census. The most common income band is $1,000-$1,249 a week (or $52)

Lastly, let's look at data at a regional level.

The dots on the right represent each occupation listed under the Health Care and Social Assistance industry in identified under several regional partnerships.

The size of the bubble represents the number of people employed in that occupation.
Some things become immediately clear:

  • First, the centre of gravity for the industry is clearly Metropolitan Melbourne, with over 70% of the occupations in the industry.
  • Second, in any region the largest occupation is registered nurses, with 60,000 in Metropolitan Melbourne alone.

The other main occupation is in aged and disabled care.
